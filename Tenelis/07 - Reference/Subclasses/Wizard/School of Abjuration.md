# School of Abjuration

The School of Abjuration emphasizes magic that blocks, banishes, or protects. Detractors of this school say that its tradition is about denial, negation rather than positive assertion. You understand, however, that ending harmful effects, protecting the weak, and banishing evil influences is anything but a philosophical void.

**Class:** [[Wizard]] | **Source:** PHB | **Subclass Features at:** 2nd, 6th, 10th, 14th level

## Key Features
- **Abjuration Savant (2nd):** The gold and time you must spend to copy an abjuration spell into your spellbook is halved.
- **Arcane Ward (2nd):** When you cast an abjuration spell of 1st level or higher, you can simultaneously use a strand of the spell's magic to create a magical ward on yourself. The ward has hit points equal to twice your wizard level + your INT modifier. Whenever you take damage, the ward takes the damage instead. When you cast an abjuration spell of 1st level or higher, the ward regains a number of hit points equal to twice the level of the spell. The ward lasts until you finish a long rest.
- **Projected Ward (6th):** When a creature that you can see within 30 feet of you takes damage, you can use your reaction to cause your Arcane Ward to absorb that damage instead. If this damage reduces the ward to 0 hit points, the warded creature takes any remaining damage.
- **Improved Abjuration (10th):** When you cast an abjuration spell that requires you to make an ability check as a part of casting that spell (as in *counterspell* and *[[Dispel Magic|dispel magic]]*), you add your proficiency bonus to that ability check.
- **Spell Resistance (14th):** You have advantage on saving throws against spells. Furthermore, you have resistance against the damage of spells.
