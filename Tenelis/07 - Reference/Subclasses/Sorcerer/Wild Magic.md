# Wild Magic

Your innate magic comes from the wild forces of chaos that underlie the order of creation. You might have endured exposure to some form of raw magic, perhaps through a planar portal leading to Limbo, the Elemental Planes, or the mysterious Far Realm. Whatever its origin, this chaotic magic churns within you, waiting for any outlet.

**Class:** [[Sorcerer]] | **Source:** PHB | **Subclass Features at:** 1st, 6th, 14th, 18th level

## Key Features
- **Wild Magic Surge (1st):** Once per turn, the DM can have you roll a d20 immediately after you cast a sorcerer spell of 1st level or higher. If you roll a 1, roll on the Wild Magic Surge table (d100) to create a random magical effect. Some effects are beneficial, others are harmful, and some are simply bizarre.
- **Tides of Chaos (1st):** You can gain advantage on one attack roll, ability check, or saving throw. Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest, unless the DM has you roll on the Wild Magic Surge table (which regains your use of this feature).
- **Bend Luck (6th):** When another creature you can see makes an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw, you can use your reaction and spend 2 sorcery points to roll 1d4 and apply the number as a bonus or penalty to the creature's roll.
- **Controlled Chaos (14th):** Whenever you roll on the Wild Magic Surge table, you can roll twice and use either result.
- **Spell Bombardment (18th):** When you roll damage for a spell and roll the highest number possible on any of the dice, you can roll that die one additional time and add it to the damage. You can use this feature only once per turn.
