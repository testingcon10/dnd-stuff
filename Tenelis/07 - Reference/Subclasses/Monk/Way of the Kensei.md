# Way of the Kensei

The Way of the Kensei monk extends ki into chosen weapons, mastering them as extensions of the body and achieving unity between warrior and blade.

**Class:** [[Monk]] | **Source:** XGtE | **Subclass Features at:** 3rd, 6th, 11th, 17th level

## Key Features
- **Path of the Kensei (3rd):** Choose two kensei weapons (one melee, one ranged) that become monk weapons. When you make an Attack action with a kensei melee weapon, you can use a bonus action for +2 AC until your next turn (Agile Parry). When you make an Attack action with a kensei ranged weapon, you can use a bonus action to add 1d4 to the ranged weapon's damage for that attack (Kensei's Shot). You also gain proficiency with calligrapher's supplies. You choose additional weapons at 6th, 11th, and 17th level.
- **One with the Blade (6th):** Your attacks with kensei weapons count as magical for overcoming resistance and immunity. Additionally, when you hit with a kensei weapon, you can spend 1 ki point to deal an extra 1d6 damage (Deft Strike).
- **Sharpen the Blade (11th):** As a bonus action, you can spend up to 3 ki points to grant a kensei weapon that lacks a magic bonus a bonus to attack and damage rolls equal to the number of ki points spent. Lasts 1 minute.
- **Unerring Accuracy (17th):** If you miss with a monk weapon attack on your turn, you can reroll the attack roll. Once per turn.
