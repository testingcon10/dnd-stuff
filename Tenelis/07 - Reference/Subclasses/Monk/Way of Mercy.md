# Way of Mercy

Monks of the Way of Mercy learn to manipulate the life force of others to bring aid to those in need. They are wandering physicians, often wearing masks to conceal their identities and separate their personal lives from their work.

**Class:** [[Monk]] | **Source:** TCoE | **Subclass Features at:** 3rd, 6th, 11th, 17th level

## Key Features
- **Implements of Mercy (3rd):** You gain proficiency in Insight, Medicine, and the herbalism kit. You also gain a special mask that serves as your monastic identity.
- **Hand of Healing (3rd):** As an action (or replacing one attack of Flurry of Blows), spend 1 ki point to touch a creature and restore HP equal to a roll of your Martial Arts die + your Wisdom modifier.
- **Hand of Harm (3rd):** Once per turn when you hit with an unarmed strike, spend 1 ki point to deal extra necrotic damage equal to a roll of your Martial Arts die + your Wisdom modifier. The target must also succeed on a Constitution save or be poisoned until the end of your next turn.
- **Physician's Touch (6th):** Your Hand of Healing can also end one disease or one of these conditions: blinded, deafened, paralyzed, poisoned, or stunned. Your Hand of [[Harm]] no longer requires a ki point to poison the target (the poison is automatically applied on a failed save).
- **Flurry of Healing and Harm (11th):** When you use Flurry of Blows, you can replace each unarmed strike with Hand of Healing (without spending ki for the healing). When you use Hand of [[Harm]] during Flurry of Blows, you can also apply it to a second unarmed strike (still costs 1 ki per use of Hand of [[Harm]]).
- **Hand of Ultimate Mercy (17th):** As an action, touch the corpse of a creature that died within the last 24 hours and spend 5 ki points. The creature returns to life with 4d10 + your Wisdom modifier HP. Usable once per long rest.
