# Fey Wanderer

A Fey Wanderer ranger's mysterious connection to the Feywild grants them the ability to beguile and deceive. Their fey magic carries both the beguiling charm and the dread of the Feywild.

**Class:** [[Ranger]] | **Source:** TCoE | **Subclass Features at:** 3rd, 7th, 11th, 15th level

## Key Features
- **Dreadful Strikes (3rd):** When you hit a creature with a weapon, you can deal an extra 1d4 psychic damage (1d6 at 11th level). Each creature can only take this extra damage once per turn.
- **Fey Wanderer Magic (3rd):** You learn additional spells that don't count against your spells known: [[Charm Person|charm person]] (3rd), [[Misty Step|misty step]] (5th), [[Dispel Magic|dispel magic]] (9th), [[Dimension Door|dimension door]] (13th), mislead (17th).
- **Otherworldly Glamour (3rd):** You can add your Wisdom modifier to any Charisma check you make. You also gain proficiency in one of the following: Deception, Performance, or Persuasion.
- **Beguiling Twist (7th):** When a creature you can see within 120 feet succeeds on a saving throw against being charmed or frightened, you can use your reaction to force a different creature within 120 feet to make a Wisdom save or be charmed or frightened by you (your choice) for 1 minute.
- **Fey Reinforcements (11th):** You learn [[Summon Fey|summon fey]] (it doesn't count against spells known). You can cast it once without a spell slot and without material components. When you cast it this way, the fey is a Fey Wanderer with special bonuses. Usable free once per long rest.
- **Misty Wanderer (15th):** You can cast [[Misty Step|misty step]] without expending a spell slot a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier per long rest. When you cast [[Misty Step|misty step]], you can bring along one willing creature within 5 feet.
