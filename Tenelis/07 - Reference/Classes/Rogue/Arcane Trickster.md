# Arcane Trickster

Some rogues enhance their fine-honed skills of stealth and agility with magic, learning tricks of enchantment and illusion. These rogues include pickpockets and burglars, but also pranksters, mischief-makers, and a significant number of adventurers.

**Class:** [[Rogue]] | **Source:** PHB | **Subclass Features at:** 3rd, 9th, 13th, 17th level

## Key Features
- **Spellcasting (3rd):** You gain the ability to cast wizard spells, using Intelligence as your spellcasting ability. Your spells must be chosen from the enchantment and illusion schools on the wizard spell list, except at certain levels where you can choose from any school. You learn three cantrips including *[[Mage Hand|mage hand]]*, and you learn spells according to the Arcane Trickster Spellcasting table.
- **Mage Hand Legerdemain (3rd):** When you cast *[[Mage Hand|mage hand]]*, you can make the hand invisible. You can use it to stow or retrieve objects in containers, pick locks with [[Thieves' Tools|thieves' tools]], or disarm traps at range. You can perform one of these tasks without being noticed by making a [[Sleight of Hand]] check contested by the creature's [[Perception]].
- **Magical Ambush (9th):** If you are hidden from a creature when you cast a spell on it, the creature has disadvantage on any saving throw it makes against the spell on that turn.
- **Versatile Trickster (13th):** You can use a bonus action to designate a creature within 5 feet of your *[[Mage Hand|mage hand]]*. You have advantage on attack rolls against that creature until the end of the turn.
- **Spell Thief (17th):** When a creature casts a spell that targets you or includes you in its area of effect, you can use your reaction to force the creature to make a save against your spell save DC. On a failure, you negate the spell's effect on you and steal it, gaining the ability to cast it for the next 8 hours. The creature can't cast that spell until the 8 hours have passed. Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.
