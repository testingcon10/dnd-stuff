# Order of Scribes

Wizards of the Order of Scribes are devoted to the preservation and mastery of magical knowledge through their spellbooks. Their spellbooks become awakened, semi-sentient companions that aid in their arcane pursuits.

**Class:** [[Wizard]] | **Source:** TCoE | **Subclass Features at:** 2nd, 6th, 10th, 14th level

## Key Features
- **Wizardly Quill (2nd):** As a bonus action, conjure a Tiny quill with the following benefits: it doesn't require ink, reduces the time to copy a spell into your spellbook to 2 minutes per spell level, and you can erase anything you write with it as a bonus action (within 5 feet).
- **Awakened Spellbook (2nd):** Your spellbook gains sentience and provides three benefits: you can use it as a spellcasting focus, when you cast a wizard spell with a damage type, you can change that type to a type from another spell in your spellbook (of the same level), and once per long rest you can cast a ritual spell in its normal casting time (instead of adding 10 minutes).
- **Manifest Mind (6th):** As a bonus action, cause the mind of your spellbook to manifest as a Tiny spectral object (ghostly tome, constellation, etc.) within 60 feet. It sheds dim light in 10 feet, and you can see and hear through it. When you cast a wizard spell, you can cast it as though you were in the object's space. When you do, you expend a spell slot 1 level higher than normal. Usable proficiency bonus times per long rest.
- **Master Scrivener (10th):** Once per long rest during a short or long rest, you can create a spell scroll of a 1st- or 2nd-level spell from your spellbook. The scroll is cast at one spell level higher than normal, and you can craft it in the reduced time of your Wizardly Quill. The scroll lasts until you use this feature again.
- **One with the Word (14th):** You gain advantage on Arcana checks. If you drop to 0 HP while your Manifest Mind is active, you can cause the mind to prevent all damage, teleport you to its space, and end Manifest Mind. Instead, spells are randomly erased from your spellbook (3d6 levels' worth). If you don't have enough spells to cover the cost, you die.
